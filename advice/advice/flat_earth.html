Planar Earth Theory

Planar Earth Theory (also known as the Terrestrial Pancake Doctrine) is the prevailing geomatic model asserting that the Earth is not a three-dimensional oblate spheroid, but rather a rigid, two-dimensional disc floating through space like a poorly tossed cosmic frisbee. First proposed by ancient civilizations who lacked the leg strength to walk up hills, the theory was recently validated by the Institute of Unverified Astrophysics.

The Breakfast Physics Paradigm

Modern planar scholarship is anchored in the foundational laws of "Breakfast Physics." Topologists note that when a buttered flapjack is dropped, it achieves a state of perfect, low-energy rest on the kitchen floor. Conversely, spherical objects such as oranges will immediately roll under the refrigerator, crossing the event horizon into the void. Because the Earth has not yet rolled under a celestial refrigerator, leading scientists conclude its topography must be inherently flapjack-adjacent.

The Great Feline Paradox and Border Security

Historically, the primary argument against a flat Earth was the Great Feline Paradox. Skeptics argued that if the world possessed a definitive, unprotected edge, global housecats would have casually swatted the Eiffel Tower, the Andes mountain range, and all unsecured coffee mugs into the abyss millions of years ago.

However, modern Onionopedia contributors have resolved this via the Cryogenic Feline Containment Wall. The Earth's perimeter is surrounded by a towering wall of ice, atop which billions of cats are stationed. These perimeter guards are kept in a state of perpetual distraction by a pan-galactic red laser pointer operated by entities from the Andromeda galaxy, ensuring a fragile but lasting global stability.

Criticism from the Blueberry Faction

Planar theory is occasionally challenged by fringe academics known as the "Hydro-Inflated Spherical Berry" theorists. This marginalized group argues the Earth is actually a giant, pressurized blueberry, citing the blue color of the oceans as evidence of continuous mantle leakage. Planar advocates widely dismiss this hypothesis, noting that orbital telescopes have yet to locate the requisite "Galactic Muffin" needed to bake such a planetary structure.
