Overview

You are participating in a decision-making experiment. You will report your beliefs about certain things. Your earnings will depend on the beliefs you report across four tasks: a weight estimation task, a height estimation task, an urns task, and a song ranking task. The weight and height tasks each contain one question; the urns and song ranking tasks each contain two questions, for a total of six questions. You will answer each question by allocating tokens across a set of possible answers. The possible answers are grouped in categories called "bins".

AT THE START OF THE SESSION, you will RANDOMLY be assigned to receive advice from one of three groups: Algorithmic Advice or Human Advice, or No Advice.

There are 2 rounds for each question. Each question will start with round 1. After you have completed round 1, you may or may not purchase and receive advice. Then you can proceed to round 2. Some tasks feature multiple questions; in those cases, you will complete round 1 for all questions in that task before moving on to round 2.

Round 1 - Initial or Pre-Beliefs

For each question, you will give a report about what you believe to be the correct answer. You will use a slider interface as illustrated in the example below to allocate {{ num_tokens }} tokens across a set of possible outcomes in 10 bins. For this example, you would report your beliefs about what percentage of the Earth's surface is covered in water. The first bin has an interval of 0% to 10%, the second bin has an interval of 11%-20%, and so on. The 10th bin represents the outcome of 91%-100%. The tokens you allocate to each bin will reflect your beliefs about the percentage of the earth's surface is covered in water.

You must allocate all 100 tokens.

The more tokens you place on the correct outcome, the more points you earn. You will receive a cash payment based on the points you earn for reporting your beliefs. Each report pays between $0 and $50. For each report, your points determine your chance of earning $50. The more points you earn, the higher your chance of earning $50. Therefore, your best strategy to maximize your earnings is to always allocate your tokens honestly according to your true beliefs.

Allocation

Suppose you think there is a fair chance that the percentage of the Earth's surface that is covered in water is between 55%-75%, then as seen in this example, you might allocate 30 tokens to the interval corresponding to 51%-60%, 30 tokens to 61%-70%, and 40 tokens to the 71%-80% interval. After you have made your selection, confirm your choices by clicking on the Submit button. This display illustrates that if the percentage of the Earth's surface that is covered in water fell in the interval 71%-80%, you would earn 73 points, which would later be translated into cash payments.

Advice Phase

After completing Round 1, you will have the opportunity to purchase advice before making your final decision. You can only purchase advice if you were assigned to the Algorithmic or Human advice conditions. You will be shown a price list with varying costs for advice. You must make a choice in every row about whether you want to buy the advice at that specific price.

If you purchase advice, the cost will be deducted from your $10.00 advice fund, called an endowment. If you are in the No Advice group, you will simply move to round 2.

Round 2 - Final or Post-beliefs

After the advice phase, you will have the opportunity to update your beliefs. You will once again allocate {{ num_tokens }} tokens across 10 bins. You may change your token allocation based on the advice you received, or you may keep it exactly the same.

Payment

Your total payment consists of three parts:

  • Participation Fee: You have already earned $10 show-up fee for agreeing to participate in this experiment, which will be paid in cash at the end of the session.
  • Endowment: You are given a ${{ endowment }} endowment to cover the cost of purchasing advice across all 4 tasks. Any money left over in from your endowment at the end of the experiment is yours to keep.
  • Task Earnings: At the end of the experiment, we will look at how accurately you allocated your tokens. Based on your score, you can earn up to $50 per report. Remember, however, that only one report will randomly be selected for payment.
