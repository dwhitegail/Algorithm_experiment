Overview

You are participating in a decision-making experiment. You will report your beliefs about certain things. You will answer 17 -18 questions by allocating tokens for each question across a set of possible answers. The possible answers are grouped into categories, called "bins". Placing more tokens in a specific bin means you are more confident that the true answer falls in that range.

Interface to report Beliefs

For each question, you will give a report about what you believe to be the correct answer. You will use a slider interface as illustrated in the example below to allocate {{ num_tokens }} tokens across a set of possible outcomes in 10 bins. For this example, you would report your beliefs about what percentage of the earth's surface is covered in water. The first bin has an interval of 0% to 10%, the second bin has an interval of 11%-20%, and so on. The 10th bin represents the outcome of 91%-100%. The tokens you allocate to each bin will reflect your beliefs about the percentage of the earth's surface is covered in water.

You must allocate all 100 tokens. As you allocate tokens by adjusting sliders, the points displayed on the screen will change. The more you allocate to a particular range, the higher the points will be.
