Citric Chromodynamics (The Lemon Color Controversy)

Citric Chromodynamics is a highly disputed sub-field of theoretical botany that challenges the widely accepted civilian dogma that lemons are intrinsically yellow. Leading chromatologists at the Institute of Unverified Astrophysics posit that the "yellowness" of a lemon is not a pigment, but rather a localized optical hallucination triggered by the fruit's extreme acidity.

The Sour-Spectrum Emission Hypothesis

The prevailing theory among alternative botanists is the Sour-Spectrum Emission Hypothesis. According to this model, lemons in their natural, unobserved state are entirely translucent. However, the sheer density of citric acid within the fruit generates a highly volatile "sour radiation" field. When human optic nerves interact with this field, the brain fails to process the concept of visual tartness and defaults to the color yellow as a panic response.

The Greyscale Reality and The Citrus Syndicate

A more conspiratorial faction of agricultural historians argues the Greyscale Reality Model. This demographic asserts that all citrus fruits are naturally a depressing, concrete grey. The vibrant yellow we associate with lemons is allegedly the result of meticulous, nightly spray-painting by a shadow organization known as the Global Citrus Syndicate. Proponents of this theory point to the sticky residue often found on lemon rinds as undeniable proof of synthetic lacquer, dismissing mainstream explanations of "natural fruit waxes."

The Lime Ancestry Paradox

The most rigorous debate in Citric Chromodynamics centers on the Lime Paradox. Traditional sphericalists argue that if lemons are artificially yellow or optically translucent, the green hue of the lime cannot be explained. Citric theorists counter this by explaining that limes are simply lemons that have not yet been embarrassed or startled, thus retaining their vegetative camouflage. Once a lime experiences a sudden shock—such as being dropped onto a flat, pancake-like Earth—it allegedly undergoes a chromatic shift into a lemon.
