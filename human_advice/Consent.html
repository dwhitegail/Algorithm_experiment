Informed Consent

Title: Beliefs on Decision-Making Tasks
Principal Investigator: Glenn Harrison, Maurice R. Greenberg School of Risk Science and Center for the Economic Analysis of Risk, Georgia State University
Student Principal Investigator: Gail Dennis-White, Maurice R. Greenberg School of Risk Science, Georgia State University


Purpose of the Study

You are invited to participate in a research study about decision-making and judgments under uncertainty. The purpose of this study is to better understand how individuals make decisions and evaluate information. Your role in the study will last no more than 150 minutes today. If you decide to take part, you will complete a computerized experiment consisting of four decision tasks with related questions. Before starting a task, you will be given instructions that describe the task in greater detail. The decision tasks should take you less than 90 minutes. You will move through the tasks at your own pace and not interact with other participants.

During the experiment you are not to speak with other participants. If you have a question, please raise your hand and an experimenter will come to speak with you. You must silence your phone and not use it for the duration of the experiment. You are not permitted to eat or drink in the computer laboratory. You must be at least 18 years of age to participate in this experiment.

Compensation

You will receive $5 for showing up for this study. You will also earn an additional amount of money for completing the decision tasks.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation is completely voluntary. You may stop participating at any time without penalty. If you do not complete all parts of the experiment, then you will only receive $5. You must complete the full experiment to receive any additional money from the decision tasks.

Confidentiality

Your responses will be kept confidential to the extent permitted by law. No personally identifying information will be reported in research findings.

Contact Information

If you have questions about this study, please contact Glenn Harrison at gharrison@gsu.edu
